Objective

 

Connect the customer's data from SQL Server to Microsoft Outlook

 

In this exercise, you will

  • Connect the new Customer list to Microsoft Outlook
Connecting the new Customer list to Microsoft Outlook
1.
Using Internet Explorer, open your island's SharePoint site at http://[island].msc.com/sites/[department], where [island] is the name of your island and [department] is your department name.
2.
From the site, click Site Settings | Site Actions section | Manage Site Features.
To connect an external list to Outlook (or other Office products), the "Offline Synchronization for External Lists" feature needs to be turned on.
3.
Scroll down to the Offline Synchronization for External Lists feature and click Activate if it is not already activated.
4.
Navigate back to the external list by clicking Customers in the Lists section of the Quick Launch.
5.
Click the LIST tab in the ribbon.
6.
Click Connect to Outlook in the Connect & Export group of the ribbon.
If the "Connect to Outlook" button is disabled, press <F5> to refresh the page and try again.
7.
If this is the first time connecting SharePoint 2013 with MS Office 2013, SharePoint will install a Microsoft Office customization. Follow the prompts and click Install.
8.
Click Close once the installer is done.
9.
Outlook will open to the new MSC Customer's contact list. Browse around a bit to see what customer information is available.
Outlook will also add a menu item in the footer linking to this and future SharePoint content.
Congratulations! You just connected a SQL Server table to an MS Outlook contacts list.

This is the end of the exercise.
