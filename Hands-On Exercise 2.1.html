Hands-On Exercise 2.1
Navigating to Your Department Site
Exercise list …








Objective
The IT department has created a site collection for each department in the islands in which MSC operates. You are the site administrator for your department—responsible for modifying the site to meet your department’s needs.

In this exercise, you will

  • Navigage to your department’s SharePoint site collection top level site from IE to support communication and collaboration
  • Create a blog subsite from Designer
Navigating to your site
1.
Using Internet Explorer, navigate to the MSC portal SharePoint site at http://home.msc.com.
To the right of the address bar in IE, you can press the Home button, which has been set to the MSC portal site.
2.
Click your island icon to open your island's portal site.
Your island portal site can also be accessed via http://[island].msc.com where [island] is the name of your island.
3.
From your island portal site, click your department's top-level site.
Your department can also be accessed via http://[island].msc.com/sites/[dept], where [island] is the name of your island and [dept] is the name of your department.
4.
If your Favorites bar is not showing, right-click the top of IE and select Favorites bar from the pull-down list. After the Favorites bar is showing, click the Add to favorites bar button in the top left of your browser to create a button in the Favorites bar for your site.
Use the Favorites button in Internet Explorer.
Creating a department blog site using SharePoint Designer
5.
Start SharePoint Designer by clicking Windows Start menu | All Programs | Microsoft Office 2013 | SharePoint Designer 2013.
If prompted, enter user name and initials, and then click OK.
6.
Click the Open Site button.
7.
In the Open Site dialog box, enter a Site Name of http://[island].msc.com/sites/[department] and click Open.
If prompted, enter a user name of [island].[department] and the password pw and then click OK.
This will take you to the information page of the home site. If the site information page is not displayed, close and open SharePoint Designer to refresh the page.
8.
Make sure the SITE tab is selected in the ribbon, then click the Subsite icon in the New section of the ribbon.
9.
In the "Specify the location of the new Web site" field of the New dialog, enter blog for the site URL so that the address of the site is: http://[island].msc.com/sites/[department]/blog
10.
Select Blog as the template and click OK.

A new instance of SharePoint Designer will open, with the new site just created and a summary page of site information displayed.

 

SharePoint Designer can display only one site, or subsite, at a time. If you want to work with multiple sites at the same time, you need to have multiple copies (instances) of SharePoint Designer open.

11.
Close the previous instance of SharePoint Designer to avoid confusion about which site we are working on.
You may want to maximize SharePoint Designer so that it is easier to work with.
12.
In the Site Information section, click Blog and enter [Department] blog in the text box. Then press <Enter>.
13.
Click the Save icon in the Quick Access Toolbar at the top left of SharePoint Designer (you can also save by pressing <Ctrl><S>).
14.
In the Customizationsection, click the Edit site home page link.
A default.aspx tab displaying the default.aspx page will open in SharePoint Designer.
Notice that you can only view your pages in Code view in Designer 2013, so most page editing should be done in the browser. We'll revisit code view in a future exercise.
15.
Right-click the default.aspx tab and select Close.
16.
Let's take a look at our blog site. Click Preview in Browser from the Manage section of the ribbon.
17.
Close Internet Explorer.
18.
Close SharePoint Designer.
Congratulations! You have built two sites for your department using both the web browser and SharePoint Designer.

This is the end of the exercise.
